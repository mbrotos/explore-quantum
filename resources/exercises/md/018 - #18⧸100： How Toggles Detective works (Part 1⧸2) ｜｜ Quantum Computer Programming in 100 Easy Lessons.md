# Exercise

(a) Let $I$ be 2-qubit-manipulation operation that literally does nothing to the two qubits. Draw the paths diagram and matrix for $I$.

(b) Suppose $U$ is a $4 \times 4$ real matrix.$^2$ We think of $U$’s columns/rows as being labeled by 2-bit strings, even though $U$ *might* not correspond to a valid quantum transformation. But suppose that $U^\dagger \cdot U = I$. Show that $U$ has the unitary property.

I require your proof to involve illustrative pictures in which a paths diagram with 1 top node and 4 bottom nodes is glued to a $4 \times 4$ paths diagram, and then this whole picture is also turned upside-down. . .

---

$^2$This problem works just the same for any $2^k \times 2^k$ matrix, but let’s stick to $k = 2$ just for concreteness.
