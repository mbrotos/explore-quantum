# Quantum coin flipping and unflipping

- Shows that applying `Hadamard` to `0` creates a 50/50 measurement outcome, and applying it to `1` also gives 50/50 but with a different sign pattern.
- Emphasizes that two states can look identical when measured even though their underlying amplitudes differ.
- Demonstrates `Hadamard` twice in a row: starting from `0` returns to `0`, and starting from `1` returns to `1`.
- Uses this to refute the idea that Hadamard is just "randomize the bit"; the intermediate superposition is a real physical state, not hidden classical uncertainty.
- Key takeaway: superpositions are not ordinary randomness, because later operations can reveal phase information and "unflip" the apparent coin toss.
