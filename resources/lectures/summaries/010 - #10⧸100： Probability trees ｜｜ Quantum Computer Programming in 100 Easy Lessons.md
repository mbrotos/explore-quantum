# Probability trees

- The lecture briefly steps away from quantum mechanics to review probabilistic computation using a toy instruction that randomly toggles a bit with probability one third.
- A probability tree tracks every execution branch, multiplies probabilities along each path, and then sums matching leaves to get the final output distribution.
- This classical analysis is presented as the direct template for quantum analysis: build the same tree, but replace probabilities with amplitudes.
- In the quantum version, path amplitudes are multiplied along branches and then added at matching outputs, which allows cancellation when positive and negative amplitudes meet.
- Key takeaway: probability trees are the bridge to amplitude trees, where the familiar rules of branching stay the same but interference makes the quantum case fundamentally more powerful.
