# What you will dislike about this course

- The course will mostly avoid physics details, hardware engineering, and vendor-specific quantum languages, focusing instead on the abstract mathematical model of quantum computation.
- The instructor warns that quantum computers are not known to magically speed up most classical problems; the headline wins are mainly factoring, some structured number-theory tasks, and Grover-style square-root speedups.
- Even so, quantum computing matters because it changes our understanding of what computation is physically possible in a quantum universe.
- A major reason to study it is that only quantum computers can directly solve quantum-input or quantum-output problems, such as manipulating qubit states or simulating quantum systems naturally.
- Key takeaway: the field is worth learning less because it solves everything faster, and more because it reveals the true computational rules of the physical world.
