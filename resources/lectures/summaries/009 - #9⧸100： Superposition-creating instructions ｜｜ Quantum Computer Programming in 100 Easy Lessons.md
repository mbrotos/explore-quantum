# Superposition-creating instructions

- The lecture introduces the genuinely quantum gates, starting with the Hadamard gate, which sends `|0>` to an equal positive superposition and `|1>` to a superposition with a negative sign on `|1>`.
- Hadamard is the key gate because it turns basis states into balanced superpositions and provides the raw material for quantum algorithms.
- The negative sign matters even though measurement squares amplitudes, because amplitudes later combine, add, and cancel when multiple instructions are composed.
- Additional examples like controlled-Hadamard and rotation-style one-qubit gates are shown to emphasize that quantum instructions can be described by path diagrams and matrices with real amplitudes, not just 0s and 1s.
- Key takeaway: quantum power starts when gates create superpositions whose amplitudes can interfere, especially through the use of positive and negative contributions.
