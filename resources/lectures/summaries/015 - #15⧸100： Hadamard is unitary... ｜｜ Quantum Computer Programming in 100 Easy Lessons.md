# Hadamard is unitary...

- Verifies directly that Hadamard preserves total squared amplitude, so it satisfies the unitary property.
- Derives the practical rule: if the old amplitudes are `x` and `y`, then Hadamard sends them to `((x+y)/sqrt(2), (x-y)/sqrt(2))`.
- Reframes Hadamard as "zero gets the sum, one gets the difference," which is more useful than memorizing only its action on `0` and `1`.
- Mentions a broader idea: valid quantum operations have undo operations, and reversing the transformation leads to that inverse.
- Key takeaway: Hadamard’s power comes from mixing amplitudes by sums and differences while still preserving a valid quantum state.
