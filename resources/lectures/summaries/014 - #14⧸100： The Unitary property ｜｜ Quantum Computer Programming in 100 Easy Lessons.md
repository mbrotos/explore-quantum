# The Unitary property

- Introduces the unitary property: valid quantum operations preserve the total sum of squared amplitudes.
- Connects this preservation rule to physics, stating that exactly the transformations with this property are physically possible quantum operations.
- Explains why classical reversible gates are unitary: they simply permute amplitudes without changing their squared total.
- Notes that once primitive instructions are unitary, any longer program built from them is unitary too.
- Key takeaway: unitarity is the rule that separates legal quantum transformations from arbitrary matrices, and reversibility is why classical gates fit naturally into quantum computing.
