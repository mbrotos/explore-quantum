# Hadamard's unitary even w⧸ other qubits

- Checks that Hadamard still preserves total squared amplitude when applied to one qubit inside a larger multi-qubit state.
- Groups basis states into pairs that differ only in the qubit being acted on, while the other qubits stay fixed.
- Applies the same Hadamard slogan pairwise: each pair transforms by sum and difference, independently of the rest of the system.
- Shows that preserving squared amplitude on each pair implies preservation for the whole multi-qubit state.
- Key takeaway: single-qubit quantum gates inside larger systems are analyzed by pairing matching contexts, not by treating the whole state as mysterious.
